Writing Samples

My Screenplay (Untitled Script, 2000)


A few pages in we get to know the main character and his problem.

FADE IN:

INT.LIVING ROOM - DAY

Darrell has on some clothes. He looks at the front door twice.

DARRELL CHANDLER: I must get rid of the body.

He marches over to the WOMAN on the floor.

Darrell grabs her legs. Slides her body across the floor.

He passes by the door. Darrell peeks through the peephole on the door. He makes a strange face.

DARRELL CHANDLER: Oh Shit!

There is a pounding at the front door now. It grows louder and louder.

He puts his dirty clothes on the dead woman's body.

EXT.HOUSE-DAY

A person is standing by the front door. She is Darrell Chandler's Mother, ESTELLE.

She walks on the lawn and tries to peek through the windows.

ESTELLE CHANDLER: Why did I tell this kid to put up curtains?

[.....]

Things get worse for Darrell Chandler.

Untitled (2002)

FADE IN:

INT.BAR-DAY

A man is sitting on a stool by the bar and sipping back on some hard liquor. He stares ahead.

MAN: It is no surprise that I do things that I'm not allowed to do. It is part of the job. I was hired to do a job and I can't upset these peope. Or myself.

He continues to look ahead.

MAN: That is why I'm sitting here drinking down my favorite hard liquor. Wishing I was doing something better with my time. But I'm not. It is noon. The lunch crowd will be here any minute to start up some shit with me. I know this.

The bartender comes by and refills his glass. The man nods. Takes a sip. Sets it down on the bar.

[...]

ANOTHER MAN enters the bar but the man at the bar doesn't notice. The other guy goes to the jukebox and slides the quarter in. Pushes some buttons.

A ROCK TUNE blasts.

The other guy goes to the other side of the man and puts his hand on his shoulder.
